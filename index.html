FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Flight Training in Kenya

Everything you need to know about becoming a pilot at Standards Aviation, Nairobi.

How much does it cost to get a Private Pilot License (PPL) in Kenya?

At Standards Aviation, the minimum cost for a Private Pilot License (PPL) is KSH 1,203,000. This includes 40 minimum flying hours on a Cessna 172 at KSH 27,000 per hour (KSH 1,080,000), ground school fees of KSH 120,000, and a registration fee of KSH 3,000. The PPL course duration is typically 4 to 8 months, depending on your schedule and weather conditions.

How much does a Commercial Pilot License (CPL) cost in Kenya?

The minimum cost for a Commercial Pilot License (CPL) at Standards Aviation is KSH 4,500,000. This includes 160 minimum flying hours on a Cessna 172 at KSH 27,000 per hour (KSH 4,320,000) and CPL ground school fees of KSH 180,000. The CPL prepares you for a career as a professional pilot with airlines and charter companies across Africa.

How long does it take to become a pilot in Kenya?

You can earn your Private Pilot License (PPL) in 4 to 8 months at Standards Aviation. Training is conducted on a one-on-one student-to-instructor basis, so you can learn at your own pace. The duration for CPL and ATPL depends on individual progress and total flying hours accumulated. Our structured programme ensures you complete your training within the stipulated timelines.

What pilot training courses do you offer?

Standards Aviation offers five comprehensive pilot training programmes:

  • Private Pilot License (PPL) – Your first step into aviation
  • Commercial Pilot License (CPL) – For aspiring professional pilots
  • Airline Transport Pilot License (ATPL) – Highest level of pilot certification
  • Flight Instructor Rating (FIR) – Become a certified flight instructor
  • Foreign License Conversion – Convert your international pilot license to a Kenyan license

What aircraft does Standards Aviation use for flight training?

We operate a fleet of well-maintained Cessna aircraft, the world's most popular training platform:

  • Cessna 172 Skyhawk (5Y-YYC, 5Y-YYA, 5Y-BYY) – Four-seat, single-engine aircraft ideal for PPL and CPL training
  • Cessna 152 (5Y-YYE) – Two-seat trainer perfect for introductory flights and early-stage training

All aircraft are maintained by our professional, licensed aircraft engineers to ensure maximum safety and airworthiness.

Where is Standards Aviation located?

Standards Aviation Flying School is located at Wilson Airport, along Langata Road, Nairobi, Kenya. Wilson Airport is one of the busiest general aviation airports in Africa, providing an ideal real-world training environment. We welcome students from across Kenya, East Africa, and the entire African continent.

Can international students enroll at Standards Aviation?

Yes! We welcome students from across Africa and around the world. Nairobi is well connected by international flights, and Wilson Airport provides an excellent training environment. We also offer a Foreign License Conversion programme for pilots who already hold an international license and wish to obtain a Kenyan pilot certificate. Contact us to discuss your specific requirements.
